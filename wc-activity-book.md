# World Cup 2026: The Ultimate Stadium Activity & Fact Book

**Target Age:** 5-10 years
**Purpose:** A companion book for the World Cup Series, featuring facts, puzzles, and creative prompts for all 11 host stadiums.

---

## Part 1: The Stadium Giants Fact File

### 1. MetLife Stadium (East Rutherford, NJ)
- **Nickname:** The Silver Giant
- **Cool Fact:** It took 40,000 tons of steel to build this stadium—that’s as heavy as 8,000 elephants!
- **Activity Prompt:** Draw yourself standing in the center of the field with 82,500 fans cheering for you!

### 2. Lincoln Financial Field (Philadelphia, PA)
- **Nickname:** The Linc
- **Cool Fact:** The Linc has over 11,000 solar panels and 14 wind turbines. It makes enough clean energy to power every single game!
- **Activity Prompt:** Design a "Super Scarf" for Leo with symbols of Philly and soccer balls.

### 3. FedExField (Landover, MD)
- **Nickname:** The FedEx Giant
- **Cool Fact:** This stadium is one of the largest in the USA and has hosted some of the biggest concerts and soccer matches in history!
- **Activity Prompt:** Draw the FedEx Giant wearing a cape made of all the world’s flags.

### 4. Hard Rock Stadium (Miami, FL)
- **Nickname:** Hard Rock Hero
- **Cool Fact:** The giant white roof covers 90% of the seats to keep fans cool in the Florida sun.
- **Activity Prompt:** Color the Hard Rock Hero with neon pink, electric blue, and sunny yellow patterns.

### 5. Mercedes-Benz Stadium (Atlanta, GA)
- **Nickname:** The Benz
- **Cool Fact:** The roof opens and closes like the petals of a giant metal flower!
- **Activity Prompt:** Draw the "Halo Board"—a giant circular screen that goes all the way around the top of the stadium.

### 6. NRG Stadium (Houston, TX)
- **Nickname:** NRG Neo
- **Cool Fact:** This was the first professional football stadium in the USA to have a retractable roof.
- **Activity Prompt:** Design a "Soccer Astronaut" suit for Ravi to wear to the game.

### 7. AT&T Stadium (Arlington, TX)
- **Nickname:** Titan Tex
- **Cool Fact:** The giant video board hanging over the field is 160 feet wide—longer than a Boeing 737 airplane!
- **Activity Prompt:** Draw a "Texas-Sized" snack tray with nachos, brisket, and a giant soccer-ball cupcake.

### 8. SoFi Stadium (Inglewood, CA)
- **Nickname:** The Infinity Giant
- **Cool Fact:** The "Infinity Screen" is the only double-sided video board in the world—you can see it from both sides!
- **Activity Prompt:** Draw a "Hollywood Walk of Fame" star for your favorite soccer player.

### 9. Levi’s Stadium (Santa Clara, CA)
- **Nickname:** The Silicon Giant
- **Cool Fact:** There is a real garden on the roof called the "Faithful Farm" that grows vegetables for the stadium!
- **Activity Prompt:** Draw a soccer field where the grass is actually made of solar panels.

### 10. Lumen Field (Seattle, WA)
- **Nickname:** The Roaring Giant
- **Cool Fact:** The stadium is designed to bounce sound back onto the field, making it one of the loudest places on Earth!
- **Activity Prompt:** Draw a "Roar-O-Meter" that shows how loud the fans are cheering for Sam’s team.

### 11. Gillette Stadium (Foxborough, MA)
- **Nickname:** The Lighthouse Giant
- **Cool Fact:** The stadium has its own 22-story lighthouse that shines a light to welcome fans to the game.
- **Activity Prompt:** Draw Nora sailing a soccer ship toward the Lighthouse Giant’s lantern.
