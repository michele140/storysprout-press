# The Lost Little Sheep

## Character Descriptions

*   **The Shepherd**: A kind man with a warm smile and a sturdy wooden staff. He wears a simple tan robe and a woven belt. He has very gentle hands and a soft voice.
*   **Daisy**: The Little Lamb. She is the smallest and fluffiest sheep in the flock. She has a pink nose and very curious, bright eyes.
*   **The 99 Sheep**: A big, happy group of fluffy white sheep. They love to eat green grass and sleep in a big, soft pile.
*   **Pip and Peep**: Two small, cheerful blue birds. They love to fly high in the sky and sing happy songs.

---

## Story Pages

### Page 1: Title Page
**Text:** The Lost Little Sheep
**Scene Description:** The kind Shepherd is holding Daisy the Little Lamb in his arms. Daisy is nuzzling his shoulder. They are surrounded by green hills and flowers.

### Page 2
**Text:** Once there was a kind Shepherd who had one hundred fluffy white sheep. He knew each of them by name.
**Scene Description:** The Shepherd is standing in a lush green field, surrounded by many fluffy sheep. He is patting one of them on the head.

### Page 3
**Text:** The Shepherd loved his sheep very much. Every morning, he led them to the greenest grass and the coolest water.
**Scene Description:** A wide shot of the flock following the Shepherd down a gentle hill toward a sparkling blue stream.

### Page 4
**Text:** Among the sheep was a tiny lamb named Daisy. Daisy was very small and very, very curious.
**Scene Description:** A close-up of Daisy. She is sniffing a bright yellow flower. Her wool looks incredibly soft and puffy.

### Page 5
**Text:** While the other sheep ate their grass, Daisy liked to look at the butterflies and the ladybugs.
**Scene Description:** Daisy is hopping after a colorful butterfly. The other sheep are in the background, peacefully grazing.

### Page 6
**Text:** One afternoon, Daisy saw a beautiful blue bird named Pip. Pip was flying toward a high, rocky hill.
**Scene Description:** Daisy is looking up at Pip the bird, who is chirping and flying away from the flock.

### Page 7
**Text:** “I wonder what is over that hill,” Daisy thought. She began to walk away from the other sheep.
**Scene Description:** Daisy is walking up a small path toward the rocky hill. She looks excited and adventurous.

### Page 8
**Text:** Daisy walked and walked. She saw new flowers and heard new sounds. She was having so much fun!
**Scene Description:** Daisy is in a part of the field she has never seen before. There are tall, purple flowers all around her.

### Page 9
**Text:** But soon, the sun began to go down. The sky turned a deep, dark orange.
**Scene Description:** Twilight in the fields. The shadows are getting long. Daisy is standing alone, looking a little bit worried.

### Page 10
**Text:** Back at the fold, the Shepherd began to count his sheep. “One... two... three...”
**Scene Description:** The Shepherd is standing at the entrance to a cozy stone sheepfold. The sheep are walking past him into the safety of the fold.

### Page 11
**Text:** “Ninety-eight... ninety-nine...” The Shepherd stopped. “Oh no! One is missing! Where is Daisy?”
**Scene Description:** The Shepherd is looking worried. He is holding his staff and looking back out at the empty fields.

### Page 12
**Text:** The Shepherd didn't say, “Oh well, I still have ninety-nine.” He loved Daisy too much to leave her alone.
**Scene Description:** The Shepherd is closing the gate for the ninety-nine sheep. He is picking up a small lantern.

### Page 13
**Text:** The Shepherd went back out into the dark fields. “Daisy! Daisy!” he called in his loud, kind voice.
**Scene Description:** The Shepherd is walking through the tall grass at night. His lantern is casting a warm, yellow glow.

### Page 14
**Text:** He looked behind big rocks. He looked under tall trees. But Daisy was not there.
**Scene Description:** The Shepherd is peeking into a small cave behind some rocks. He looks very determined to find his little lamb.

### Page 15
**Text:** High above, Pip and Peep heard the Shepherd calling. They flew down to help him look.
**Scene Description:** The two blue birds are flying near the Shepherd's head, chirping and pointing their wings toward the rocky hill.

### Page 16
**Text:** Daisy was cold and scared. She was stuck in some prickly bushes. “Baa! Baa!” she cried softly.
**Scene Description:** Daisy is huddled in a bush with some thorns. She looks sad, and there is a single tear in her eye.

### Page 17
**Text:** The Shepherd heard a tiny sound. He followed the sound all the way to the prickly bushes.
**Scene Description:** The Shepherd is leaning forward, listening intently. He is near the rocky hill now.

### Page 18
**Text:** “I found you, Daisy!” the Shepherd cried. He gently reached into the bushes to help her.
**Scene Description:** The Shepherd is kneeling by the bush. He is carefully moving the branches away so Daisy won't get scratched.

### Page 19
**Text:** The Shepherd lifted Daisy out of the bushes. He brushed the leaves off her wool and gave her a big hug.
**Scene Description:** A close-up of the Shepherd's hands holding Daisy. Daisy is licking his cheek.

### Page 20
**Text:** Daisy was so happy to see her Shepherd. She felt warm and safe in his strong arms.
**Scene Description:** The Shepherd is carrying Daisy like a baby. He is walking back toward the sheepfold.

### Page 21
**Text:** The Shepherd carried Daisy all the way home. He didn't make her walk because she was so tired.
**Scene Description:** The Shepherd is walking under a starry sky. Daisy is fast asleep on his shoulder.

### Page 22
**Text:** When they reached the fold, all the other sheep were happy to see Daisy. “Baa! Baa!” they cheered.
**Scene Description:** The Shepherd is putting Daisy down inside the fold. The other sheep are gathered around her, nuzzling her.

### Page 23
**Text:** The Shepherd told his friends the good news. “My lost sheep is found!” he said with a big smile.
**Scene Description:** The Shepherd is talking to some other shepherds. They are all smiling and celebrating.

### Page 24
**Text:** The Shepherd knew that every single sheep was important. And Daisy knew she would always be loved.
**Scene Description:** Daisy is sleeping peacefully in a pile of hay, surrounded by the other sheep. The Shepherd is standing by the gate, watching over them with a smile.

**The End.**
