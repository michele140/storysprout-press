# World Cup Series Book #4: Miami's Fiesta

**Target Age:** 5-9 years
**Theme:** Celebration, culture, family, vibrant energy.
**Characters:**
- **Mateo:** A lively 8-year-old from Miami with a passion for Argentinian soccer and salsa music.
- **Abuela:** Mateo’s grandmother, who brings her famous snacks and a lot of energy.
- **The Hard Rock Hero:** A flashy stadium character with neon lights, a tropical shirt pattern on his walls, and "sunglasses" made of stadium canopy.
- **Team Argentina:** The "Albiceleste" with their iconic light blue and white stripes.

## Page 2
**Visual:** Abuela’s kitchen. She is packing a cooler with empanadas and tropical fruit. Mateo is wearing his Argentina jersey and a sun hat, doing a "samba" step around the table.
**Text:**
In Miami, the sun is always shining, and there’s always a reason to celebrate! Mateo’s house was full of music and the smell of Abuela’s famous empanadas. 

“Mateo, stop dancing and help me with the cooler!” Abuela laughed, her eyes crinkling. 

“I can’t help it, Abuela! Argentina is playing at the Hard Rock Stadium today! It’s going to be a fiesta!”

## Page 3

**Text:**
They drove across the bridge, seeing the blue water sparkle like diamonds. 

“Look, Abuela! Even the boats have flags!” Mateo pointed to a sailboat flying the sun-symbol of Argentina. 

In Miami, people from all over the Caribbean and South America live together. Today, it felt like one big, happy family was heading to the game.

## Page 4

As they arrived, the "Hard Rock Hero" stood tall. He was the coolest stadium in the world! 

He wore a facade painted with bright tropical leaves and huge "sunglasses" made of his white roof canopy. He didn't just host games; he hosted parties! Mateo felt the rhythm of the stadium—a low, rhythmic beat like a drum.
