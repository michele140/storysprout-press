## BOOK 3: THE GREAT WHITE NORTH

**FORMAT:** Dork Diaries style — mix of text and black & white illustrations on every page
**TONE:** First-person, conversational, diary-like. 
**SETTING:** Alaska (Snowy mountains, cozy cabins, pine forests)

# [PAGE 2 - CHAPTER 1: LANDING IN ALASKA]

**[ILLUSTRATION: Pandy the Panda is shivering. Her breath is coming out in a little cloud doodle. She has a snowflake on her nose.]**

Dear Diary,

I have a new rule for adventures:

**RULE #10: NEVER, EVER, EVER TRAVEL TO A PLACE WHERE THE AIR TURNS YOUR BREATH INTO CLOUDS.**

Seriously. My fluff is not designed for this. I am a Bamboo Forest panda. I like sun, I like shade, and I like things that are not **FROZEN**.

One minute we were in Candyland, surrounded by chocolate volcanoes and soda waves (which was sticky, but warm!). Then Winky pushed that button, the map glitched, and—*WHOOSH*—we were falling through a swirling vortex of sparkles and ice.

And now?

Now I am a Panda-Sicle. 

**[ILLUSTRATION: A tiny doodle of Pandy inside an ice cube with the label "PANDA-SICLE".]**

---

# [PAGE 3]

**[ILLUSTRATION: The eight friends are popping out of a giant snowbank. Luna the Rabbit looks like a giant snowball with ears.]**

"IS EVERYONE ALIVE?" I shouted (or tried to, but my teeth were chattering so hard it sounded like: *Is-is e-v-very-one a-a-live?*)

*POOF!* 

Luna’s head popped out of the snow. 

"STARS! PANDY! EVERYTHING IS WHITE! AND COLD! AND SPARKLY! I THINK I’M IN RABBIT HEAVEN!" 

Luna, of course, loves it. She’s already trying to see if she can hop *on top* of the snow. 

**[ILLUSTRATION: Luna mid-hop, her long ears trailing behind her. A "BOING!" sound effect is written in the snow.]**

"HONK! I can't feel my trunk!" 

Dirty the Elephant was next. She was currently stuck upside down, her big grey legs kicking at the sky. 

**[ILLUSTRATION: Dirty’s bottom sticking out of the snow, with her legs waving frantically.]**

---

# [PAGE 4]

**[ILLUSTRATION: Winky the Pig is standing on a flat rock, looking out at the snowy mountains. She’s wearing her sunglasses, even though it’s cloudy.]**

"Alright, nobody panic!" Winky barked. She was shivering, but she was trying to look "CEO-Cool." 

"This is just a... a... geographical realignment! A market shift! We just need to find the local business district and buy some sweaters."

"Winky," Lavender said, shaking snow off her purple fur. "I don't think there *is* a business district. I think there’s just... more snow."

Apricot nodded. "And trees. And big pointy mountains."

**[ILLUSTRATION: A wide shot of the Alaskan wilderness. Huge pine trees, jagged white mountains, and a frozen lake in the distance.]**

Daisy was huddling close to Hippy. Hippy looked like a giant purple mountain himself, except he was turning a slightly lighter shade of violet from the cold. 

"I miss the chocolate swamp," Hippy rumbled. 

Me too, Hippy. Me too.

---

# [PAGE 5]

**[ILLUSTRATION: The group is walking through the deep snow. Pandy is leading the way with the map. The map is glowing with a soft blue light.]**

We started walking. Well, the others walked. I kind of waddled. 

The map was still glowing, but the word "ALASKA" was pulsing in rhythm with my heartbeat. 

"Where are we going, Pandy?" Daisy asked. She looked like a little white cloud moving across a bigger white cloud. 

"The map is pointing toward that smoke!" I said, pointing toward a thin grey curl rising from the trees. 

**[ILLUSTRATION: A tiny doodle of a log cabin with a smoking chimney in the distance.]**

"Smoke means fire," Lavender said, her clever eyes lighting up. "And fire means warmth!"

"And warmth means my ears might stop being popsicles!" Luna cheered.

---

# [PAGE 6]

**[ILLUSTRATION: The friends arrive at a charming little log cabin. It has a sign over the door that says: "THE COZY PAW CAFE". Warm yellow light is spilling out of the windows.]**

We reached the cabin just as the sun started to go down. The air was getting even colder, which I didn't think was possible. 

The sign looked so friendly. It had a little carving of a bear holding a teapot. 

"Okay, Winky," I whispered. "You’re the boss of talking. You go first."

Winky adjusted her sunglasses. "Step aside, amateurs. Watch a professional at work."

**[ILLUSTRATION: Winky standing on her tiptoes, reaching for the door handle. She looks very determined.]**

She pounded on the door. *THUMP! THUMP! THUMP!*

"OPEN UP! WE HAVE A BUSINESS PROPOSAL AND WE ARE VERY COLD!"

---

# [PAGE 7]

**[ILLUSTRATION: The door opens. A large, warm brown bear wearing a thick, colorful knitted scarf is standing there. She has a kind face and is holding a wooden spoon.]**

The door creaked open, and the smell of cinnamon and pine needles hit us like a giant hug. 

The bear looked down at us. She blinked her big, gentle eyes. 

"Oh my goodness," she said. Her voice was like velvet. "Look at all of you! You’re absolutely freezing!"

"I’m Winky, CEO of—" 

"You're a tiny pig in a very cold place," the bear interrupted, scooping Winky up. "And you're a panda! And an elephant! And... goodness, so many friends!"

**[ILLUSTRATION: The bear (Layla) gathering the whole group into the warm cabin. She looks like she’s collecting dolls.]**

"Come in, come in! I’m **Layla**. Welcome to the Cozy Paw!"

---

# [PAGE 8]

**[ILLUSTRATION: Inside the cafe. It’s filled with soft rugs, mismatched wooden chairs, and shelves full of books and jars of honey. A large fireplace is roaring in the corner.]**

Diary, the Cozy Paw Cafe is the most wonderful place in the world (besides my hollow log, obviously). 

It was so warm! I could feel my fur starting to fluff back up. 

"Sit by the fire!" Layla commanded, bustling around. "I’ll bring out the hot cocoa and the Traveler’s Blankets!"

**[ILLUSTRATION: All eight friends huddling together on a giant rug in front of the fire. They look like they are melting with happiness.]**

*AHHHH.* 

"This is better than Candyland," Hippy sighed, leaning his head against a velvet cushion. 

Even Winky didn't argue. She was too busy burying herself in a woolly blanket.

---

# [PAGE 9]

**[ILLUSTRATION: Layla placing a tray of steaming mugs and a plate of cookies on a low table. She looks at them with pure fascination.]**

"So," Layla said, sitting down in a giant rocking chair. "I’ve lived in these mountains my whole life, and I’ve seen many travelers... but never a panda from a Bamboo Forest or a purple hippo from a swamp."

"Wait," I said, putting down my cocoa. "How do you know where we’re from?"

Layla smiled and pointed to the wall. 

**[ILLUSTRATION: A massive map of the world is pinned to the wall. It’s covered in notes, drawings, and pieces of colored string.]**

"I love stories," Layla explained. "I collect them! Every traveler who comes through these woods tells me about their home. I write them all down in my journal."

---

# [PAGE 10]

**[ILLUSTRATION: Layla holding a thick leather-bound book that says "THE TRAVELER'S JOURNAL" on the cover.]**

"I’ve heard of the Wild Meadow," Layla continued, nodding at Luna. "And the Sunny Savannah. But I’ve never seen a map like yours, Pandy."

I showed her the scroll. It was glowing a steady, peaceful blue now. 

"It’s a Shift-Map," Lavender explained, trying to sound like a scientist again. "It takes us where the adventure is."

Layla leaned in, her eyes wide. "Adventure? Oh, I hope you have plenty of that! Alaska is full of it. But first..."

She looked toward the back of the cafe. 

"OTIS! STOP TESTING THE NEW FLAVORS AND COME MEET OUR GUESTS!"

# [PAGE 11 - CHAPTER 2: MEETING OTIS]

**[ILLUSTRATION: A cream-colored bear (Otis) walks out from the kitchen. He’s wearing a messy apron and is holding an ice cream cone in each paw. He has a smudge of chocolate on his nose.]**

Dear Diary,

Meet Otis. 

Otis is... well, Otis is unique. 

"Ice cream?" he asked, offering a cone to Dirty. 

"It’s snow-flavored! I call it 'Glacier Gulp'."

**[ILLUSTRATION: Dirty taking the cone with her trunk. She looks confused.]**

"Otis," Layla sighed, but she was smiling. "They just got here from a blizzard. I think they want *hot* things, not cold things."

"Nonsense!" Otis laughed. "Ice cream is for all seasons! It’s 30 degrees outside, which means the ice cream won't melt! It’s the perfect time to eat it!"

---

# [PAGE 12]

**[ILLUSTRATION: Otis sitting on the floor with the group. He’s handing out mini-cones to everyone. Winky is looking at hers like it’s a piece of modern art.]**

"So," Otis said, taking a giant bite of his own cone. "Layla says you guys have seen the world! Is it true? Are there really places where the ground *doesn't* turn white?"

"The Bamboo Forest is green," I said. "Always green."

"And the Savannah is gold!" Dirty added.

"And Candyland is... well, it’s a rainbow," Apricot said, doing a little surfing motion with her paw. 

Otis’s eyes were like saucers. "A rainbow? That gives me an idea! Layla! We need more sprinkles! I’m going to invent 'The Candyland Crunch'!"

---

# [PAGE 13]

**[ILLUSTRATION: Otis is frantically mixing things in a big bowl back in the kitchen. Clouds of sugar and flour are flying everywhere. The friends are watching from the doorway.]**

Otis is the Lead Flavor Engineer of the Cozy Paw. 

"He’s obsessed," Layla whispered to me. "He has a thousand flavors. One for every mood, every weather, and now... one for every friend!"

*CLATTER! BANG!*

"I GOT IT!" Otis shouted. 

He ran out holding a bowl of bright pink ice cream with green sparkles and yellow dots. 

"The Aloha Swirl! In honor of our new Hawaii friends!"

**[ILLUSTRATION: Apricot and Lavender tasting the ice cream. Their ears are standing straight up.]**

"IT TASTES LIKE SUNSHINE!" Lavender screamed.

---

# [PAGE 14]

**[ILLUSTRATION: Otis looking at the Smudges (Pandy, Dirty, Luna, Daisy). He’s tapping his spoon against his chin.]**

"Now," Otis muttered. "I need something for the originals. Something... soft. Something... comforting."

He looked at me. He looked at Dirty. He looked at Daisy. 

"I have it! 'The Smudge Special'! It’ll be marshmallow fluff, vanilla bean, and a hint of... what does a panda like?"

"Bamboo," I said. 

"Right! Bamboo-flavored white chocolate!" 

**[ILLUSTRATION: A doodle of a giant ice cream sundae with a panda face on top.]**

Otis is a genius. A crazy, ice-cream-obsessed genius.

---

# [PAGE 15]

**[ILLUSTRATION: Winky pulling Otis aside. She has a notebook and a very serious face.]**

Winky, of course, saw a business opportunity. 

"Listen, Polar-Pal," Winky said, talking fast. "Your product is top-tier. High-quality, unique branding. But your distribution is a nightmare! You’re in the middle of a forest!"

"I like the forest," Otis said, licking his spoon. "The trees are good listeners."

"Forget the trees! We need to franchise! Cozy Paw Global! We’ll have a stand in JellyCat City, a kiosk in the Savannah... we’ll be the Kings and Queens of Cold!"

**[ILLUSTRATION: Winky drawing a graph that only goes UP.]**

Otis just blinked. "Can I still wear my apron?"

---

# [PAGE 16]

**[ILLUSTRATION: Pandy and Layla sitting by the window. The moon is rising over the snowy trees. It looks peaceful.]**

While Winky was "consulting" with Otis, I talked to Layla. 

"Do you ever get lonely out here, Layla?" I asked. 

Layla looked out at the mountains. "Sometimes. But then a new story walks through the door, and the world feels big and full again."

She looked at my map. 

"Pandy, stories are like your map. They connect us to places we’ve never been. And sometimes, they show us where we belong."

**[ILLUSTRATION: A tiny doodle of a heart with a book inside it.]**

I like Layla. She understands the "Dreaming of More" feeling.

---

# [PAGE 17]

**[ILLUSTRATION: The friends are all tucked into their Traveler’s Blankets. Otis has given everyone a small 'Bedtime Scoop' of lavender-honey ice cream.]**

It was time for bed. Layla set up a row of cozy baskets and rugs near the fire. 

"Tomorrow," Layla said, "we’ll show you the real Alaska. We’ll go to the Great Glacier!"

"Is there surfing?" Apricot asked sleepily. 

"Ice-surfing," Otis whispered. "But watch out for the penguins. They’re very competitive."

**[ILLUSTRATION: A doodle of a penguin on a surfboard made of ice.]**

Goodnight, Diary. I’m warm, I’m full of ice cream, and I’m... actually excited for tomorrow. 

(Don't tell Rule #10.)

**DOG SLEDDING IS THE BEST THING EVER.**

# [PAGE 21]

"How is this even possible?" Lavender asked, poking her snow-cone with a paw. 

"Alaska magic," Layla said, winking. "The lake remembers every flavor that has ever fallen into it."

"In that case," Winky said, "I’m going to drop a piece of pepperoni in there and see what happens."

**[ILLUSTRATION: Winky holding a tiny piece of pepperoni over the hole. Everyone is looking at her like she’s crazy.]**

"Winky, no!" Daisy cried. "Pepperoni ice cream sounds terrible!"

"It’s called 'Market Testing', Daisy! Look it up!"

---

# [PAGE 22]

Suddenly... *SPLUT!*

A giant snowball hit Hippy right on his purple nose. 

He blinked. He looked at Apricot. 

"Oh," Hippy rumbled. "It’s on."

**[ILLUSTRATION: Hippy using his big tail to catapult ten snowballs at once. It’s like a snowball-cannon!]**

*THWACK-THWACK-THWACK!*

"RETREAT!" Winky screamed, diving behind Dirty’s large ears. 

---

# [PAGE 23]

**[ILLUSTRATION: Pandy and Daisy are making 'Snow-Pandas' and 'Snow-Lambs' on the side. They look very cute and much more peaceful than the others.]**

Daisy and I decided to stay out of the "War of the Waffles" (which is what Otis called the snowball fight). 

"Look, Pandy!" Daisy said. "I made a snow-stuffy!"

She had built a little lamb out of snow, using pebbles for eyes and a twig for a tail. 

**[ILLUSTRATION: A tiny snow-lamb next to a tiny snow-panda. They are holding paws.]**

"It looks just like us," I said. 

"Because we're part of Alaska now," Daisy whispered. "Even if we’re just visiting."

---

# [PAGE 24]

**[ILLUSTRATION: The sky is turning green, purple, and blue. The Northern Lights are dancing over the mountains.]**

As the sun disappeared, the sky did something I’ve never seen before. 

It started to dance. 

Great ribbons of light—green, pink, and shimmering violet—swirled across the stars. 

**[ILLUSTRATION: All ten friends standing on the frozen lake, looking up at the Aurora Borealis. Their faces are lit up in different colors.]**

"The Northern Lights," Layla whispered. "The sky is telling its own story tonight."

It was so beautiful I forgot to be cold. I forgot about Winky’s plans and Otis’s ice cream. 

I just felt... small. But in a good way.

---

# [PAGE 25]

**[ILLUSTRATION: Pandy looking at the map. The map is glowing in the *exact same colors* as the Northern Lights. The green and pink ribbons are moving across the parchment.]**

I looked down at the map in my paws. 

It was pulsing. The light from the sky was being "downloaded" into the map!

"Guys!" I yelled. "Look at the map! It’s dancing too!"

Lavender ran over. "It’s syncing! The map is using the energy from the lights to find our next path!"

"Is it Hawaii again?" Apricot asked hopefully. 

"Or NYC?" Winky added. 

The map didn't answer. It just glowed brighter and brighter until the whole lake was lit up like a disco.

---

# [PAGE 26]

**[ILLUSTRATION: The group is back in the sled, heading back to the cafe. They all look exhausted but peaceful.]**

The huskies bounced us back home under the glowing sky. 

"Today was a 10 out of 10," Otis said, sharing a 'Northern Lights' popsicle with Lavender. 

"I learned that snow isn't just cold," I wrote in my diary later that night. "It’s a blank page. And we’re writing our story on it, one paw-print at a time."

**[ILLUSTRATION: A tiny doodle of a panda paw-print in the corner of the page.]**

Goodnight, Diary. Tomorrow, Layla wants to hear *our* stories. 

I hope I remember the good parts.
