# Activity Book Outline: The Good Samaritan (Bible Adventures)

**Series:** Bible Adventures (Series 2)
**Book Title:** The Good Samaritan
**Target Age:** 2-6 years
**Theme:** Kindness, helping others, being a good neighbor.
**Total Pages:** 24

---

## Page-by-Page Outline

**Page 1: Title Page**
- **Activity:** "This Book Belongs To" name plate with a small illustration of a smiling heart and a donkey.
- **Visual:** A simple frame for the name.

**Page 2: Introduction / Story Recap**
- **Activity:** Brief story recap with "Color the Heart."
- **Visual:** A large, simple heart with the words "I can be kind."

**Page 3: Coloring Page - The Traveler**
- **Activity:** Coloring.
- **Visual:** A friendly man (the traveler) walking happily down a winding road with a small bag on a stick.

**Page 4: Tracing Page - The Road**
- **Activity:** Trace the winding road.
- **Visual:** Dotted lines showing the path from Jerusalem to Jericho.

**Page 5: Coloring Page - The Kind Samaritan**
- **Activity:** Coloring.
- **Visual:** The Good Samaritan with a warm smile, carrying a jug of water.

**Page 6: Letter Tracing - K is for Kind**
- **Activity:** Trace the letter 'K' and the word "KIND."
- **Visual:** Large block letters with arrows for guidance.

**Page 7: Maze - Help the Samaritan**
- **Activity:** Maze.
- **Visual:** Help the Samaritan through the maze to reach the traveler sitting by the side of the road.

**Page 8: Coloring Page - The Friendly Donkey**
- **Activity:** Coloring.
- **Visual:** A cute, smiling donkey standing patiently.

**Page 9: Connect-the-Dots - The Water Jug**
- **Activity:** Connect dots 1-10 to reveal the jug.
- **Visual:** A simple jug shape.

**Page 10: Tracing Page - Shapes of Kindness**
- **Activity:** Trace circles and squares representing "supplies" (bread, bandages, water).
- **Visual:** Dotted shapes.

**Page 11: Coloring Page - Helping Hands**
- **Activity:** Coloring.
- **Visual:** The Samaritan putting a bandage on the traveler's arm. Gentle and sweet.

**Page 12: Counting Activity - Bandages**
- **Activity:** Count the bandages on the page (1, 2, 3, 4, 5).
- **Visual:** Five simple bandage strips scattered on the page.

**Page 13: Letter Tracing - S is for Samaritan**
- **Activity:** Trace the letter 'S' and the word "SAMARITAN."
- **Visual:** Large dotted letters.

**Page 14: Coloring Page - The Inn**
- **Activity:** Coloring.
- **Visual:** A cozy-looking inn with a friendly innkeeper at the door.

**Page 15: Matching Activity - Story Sequence**
- **Activity:** Match the picture to the number (1. Walking, 2. Helping, 3. At the Inn).
- **Visual:** Three simple icons to match to numbers.

**Page 16: Coloring Page - Riding the Donkey**
- **Activity:** Coloring.
- **Visual:** The traveler riding safely on the donkey while the Samaritan walks beside him.

**Page 17: Drawing Activity - Design a Bag**
- **Activity:** "Draw what the Samaritan has in his bag for the traveler."
- **Visual:** A large empty bag shape to draw inside (fruit, bread, etc.).

**Page 18: Tracing Page - Paths of Love**
- **Activity:** Trace wavy lines.
- **Visual:** Lines connecting the Samaritan to various people (a friend, a grandma, a neighbor).

**Page 19: Coloring Page - Everyone is Happy**
- **Activity:** Coloring.
- **Visual:** The Samaritan, the traveler, and the innkeeper all waving.

**Page 20: Counting Activity - Coins**
- **Activity:** Count the coins given to the innkeeper (1, 2).
- **Visual:** Two large, simple circles representing gold coins.

**Page 21: Drawing Activity - You are a Neighbor**
- **Activity:** "Draw yourself helping a friend today!"
- **Visual:** A large blank frame with a border of small hearts.

**Page 22: Coloring Page - The Golden Rule**
- **Activity:** Coloring.
- **Visual:** Large letters saying "Love Your Neighbor" with flowers around them.

**Page 23: Memory Verse Page**
- **Activity:** Color the words "Be Kind to One Another."
- **Visual:** Decorative border with the verse in large, easy-to-read font.

**Page 24: Certificate of Kindness**
- **Activity:** Cut out and keep.
- **Visual:** A certificate that says: "This certifies that [Name] is a KINDNESS HERO! I can help my neighbors!"
- **Illustrations:** Stars, a trophy, and a smiling donkey.

---

## Visual Notes for Illustrator
- **Consistency:** Use the same character designs from the storybook (Samaritan's blue robe, donkey's grey fur).
- **Simplicity:** Keep lines thick and areas large for easy coloring by toddlers.
- **Tone:** Everything should look happy, helpful, and safe. Avoid depicting any "bad guys" from the story; focus entirely on the act of helping.
